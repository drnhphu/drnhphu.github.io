Think Fast and Slow: The Attention Effect

Published by Dr. Hoang-Phu Nguyen | Category: Psychology

In his groundbreaking book "Thinking, Fast and Slow", Daniel Kahneman describes two different ways the brain forms thoughts: System 1 (fast, automatic, frequent, emotional, stereotypic, subconscious) and System 2 (slow, effortful, infrequent, logical, calculating, conscious).

The Role of Attention

Attention is a limited resource. When we are focused on a demanding task (using System 2), we often miss other events occurring around us. This is sometimes called the Attention Effect.

In behavioral economics, this implies that consumers or investors who are mentally depleted or distracted are much more likely to fall back on System 1 heuristics, leading to systematic biases in decision making.

"A reliable way to make people believe in falsehoods is frequent repetition, because familiarity is not easily distinguished from truth." — Daniel Kahneman

By understanding these mechanisms, we can design better interventions in both corporate governance and public policy to nudge individuals toward more optimal decisions.
